Applications of Graph Theory in Network Analysis

This paper explores how graph theory principles can be applied to analyze social networks, transportation systems, and computer networks.

Mathematical Abstract

Let G = (V, E) be a simple graph with vertex set V and edge set E. We examine properties of adjacency matrices and their eigenvalues to determine network connectivity. The paper proves that for any connected graph with n vertices, the second smallest eigenvalue of the Laplacian matrix is positive, confirming the graph's connectivity.

Dr. Robert Williams April 20, 2023
Graph Theory Networks Linear Algebra

Patterns in Prime Number Distribution

An investigation into the statistical distribution of prime numbers and the Riemann hypothesis implications.

Mathematical Abstract

This research examines the prime number theorem extension through the lens of the Riemann zeta function. We establish bounds for the error term in the prime number theorem and explore connections between nontrivial zeros of ζ(s) and prime distribution. The paper presents a new approach to understanding the irregularities in prime spacing.

Sarah Johnson April 5, 2023
Number Theory Primes Analysis
